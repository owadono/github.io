Project Design

Deep Learning-Based Potato Health Classification for Automated Segregation System

ABSTRACT

Post-harvest potato storage is often challenged by bacterial and fungal infections carried over from soil-contaminated produce, which can reduce market yield and quality. To address this issue, an automated potato health classification and segregation system was developed and assessed, with three design alternatives compared according to several constraints in domains of Functionality, Performance, Compatibility, etc., and the winning design being chosen by utilizing Pareto multi-criteria decision-making analysis. The selected design integrates a deep learning image recognition model, specifically a trained VGG-16 architecture, with an automated segregation prototype composed of a conveyor belt to transfer the potatoes, camera for classification and servo motors to remove infected potatoes after harvest. The evaluation resulted in 98.9% accuracy, indicating that the system can reliably identify diseased potatoes based on their skin health, supporting early intervention and reducing the risk of cross-contamination, and successfully segregating them to their respective containers. Overall, this approach demonstrates how automation can contribute to minimizing agricultural waste and improving the consistency of supply chains by ensuring healthier produce reaches the market.
